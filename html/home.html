POPULAR DEBATE FORMAT
Asian Parliamentary
AP format, also known as Asian Parliamentary, is the format usually used in competitions for Asian high schools and universities. Each round consist of two teams: the supporting team (Government) and the opposing team (Oppposition), each team has 3 members. Team Government has three position: Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister and Goverment Whip. Team Opposition has three positions: Leader of Opposition and Opposition Whips.

British Parliamentary
BP format, also known as British Parliamentary, is a debating format used in many formal international debating compertition involing high school as well as university institution. This format required a total of four teams seperated into two side, Government (support) and Opposition (againts) and two benches, Opening and Closing, with each bench siting in one straight row.

World School
World School debating format, obtaining its name from the World Schools Debating Championship, the largest high-school debating compertition, largly resembles AP format.Six speakers will be divided into two teams, Proposition and Opposition, 3 speakers on each team. Members will be marked P1, P2, P3 for Proposition and 1 reply speaker; O1, O2, O3 for Opposition and 1 reply speaker.
